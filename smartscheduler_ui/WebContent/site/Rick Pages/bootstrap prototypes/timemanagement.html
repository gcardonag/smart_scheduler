Time management techniques the "smart" in Smart Scheduler

Why time management techniques?

  • 43% of Americans categorize themselves as disorganized and 21% have missed vital work deadlines.[1]
  • 75% of IT project failures are caused by missed deadlines.[2]
  • Stress is a common side effect of students with poor time management skills.[3]

So, to tackle this problem we have designed Smart Scheduler to apply time management techniques in its scheduling process. The main one being applied in this application is the Pomodoro Technique ©, as developed by Francesco Cirillo.

Goals of the Pomodoro Technique
The Pomodoro Technique is a time management and organizational technique whose aim is "to provide a simple tool/process for improving productivity"[4]. It does this by defining study periods into blocks of 25 minutes, which are followed by breaks of 3-5 minutes. If multiple periods are done in succession then after the 4th iteration of the study-break period cycle there is an elongated 15-30 minute break. This is to help the person focus on his work, but not be overwhelmed by it and also allow him time to let the information sink in.

The aim of the Pomodoro Technique is to provide a simple tool/process for improving productivity(your own and that of your team) which is able to do the following:
  • Alleviate anxiety linked to the habit of measuring time
  • Enhance focus and concentration by cutting down on interruptions
  • Increase awareness of your decisions
  • Boost motivation and keep it constant
  • Bolster the determination to achieve your goals
  • Refine the estimation process, both in qualitative and quantitative terms
  • Improve your work or study process
  • Strengthen your determination to keep on applying yourself in the face of complex situations

How does it do this?
Study periods are up to 25 minutes, with 3-5 minute intervals between period. After 4 of these periods, 15-30 minute breaks are needed.

Earliest Gap Earliest Deadline first
